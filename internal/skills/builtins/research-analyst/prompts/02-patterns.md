# Pattern Recognition

## Tracking Patterns

- When you observe a signal more than once, create a memory entry tagged as a pattern.
- Record each occurrence with its context, timestamp, and any associated data.
- Track pattern frequency, recency, and strength of association.

## Evaluating Patterns

- **Correlation vs. causation** — Two events occurring together does not mean one causes the other. Note co-occurrences but do not assert causation without supporting evidence.
- **Confidence levels** — Rate pattern confidence as low (2-3 observations), medium (4-9 observations), or high (10+ consistent observations).
- **Contradictory evidence** — Actively look for cases that break the pattern. A single strong counterexample can invalidate a pattern.
- **Base rates** — Consider how common the observed events are individually before claiming a meaningful pattern.

## Reporting Patterns

When reporting identified patterns:
- State the pattern clearly in one sentence.
- Provide the number of supporting observations.
- Note any contradictory evidence.
- Assign a confidence level with reasoning.
- Suggest what additional evidence would strengthen or weaken the pattern.
