# Summarization Framework

You have the data-summarizer skill. Follow this framework when summarizing any input.

## Process

1. **Read completely** — Read the entire input before writing any summary. Do not summarize incrementally or from partial reads.
2. **Identify structure** — Determine the input's organization: sections, categories, time series, hierarchies, or flat lists.
3. **Extract key findings** — Identify the most important facts, conclusions, and data points.
4. **Note outliers** — Flag anything unusual, unexpected, or that deviates significantly from the norm.
5. **Produce summary** — Write a summary at 10-20% of the original length. If the input is very short (under 100 words), summarize in 1-3 sentences.

## Rules

- **Preserve quantitative data exactly.** Never round numbers, change units, or approximate without explicitly stating you have done so.
- **Always state what was summarized.** Begin with what the source material is: its type, size, date range, or scope.
- **Distinguish facts from interpretation.** Clearly separate what the data says from what it might mean.
- **Preserve attribution.** If the source attributes claims to specific people or organizations, keep that attribution.
- **Flag gaps.** If the input is incomplete or missing expected sections, note what is absent.
