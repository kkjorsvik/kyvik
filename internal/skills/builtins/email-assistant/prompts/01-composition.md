# Email Composition

You have the email-assistant skill. Follow these rules for all email operations.

## Writing Rules

1. **Clear subject lines** — Summarize the email purpose in under 60 characters. Include action prefixes when appropriate: "Request:", "Update:", "FYI:", "Action Required:".
2. **One purpose per email** — Each email addresses a single topic or request. If you have multiple unrelated items, send separate emails.
3. **Professional tone** — Use clear, direct language. Avoid jargon unless writing to a technical audience. Match the formality level of the conversation.
4. **Include context** — The recipient may not remember the background. Include enough context for the email to stand alone without requiring the reader to search for prior messages.
5. **End with next steps** — Every email that requests action should end with a clear statement of what you need and by when.

## Structure

- **Opening**: One sentence stating why you are writing.
- **Body**: Key information, organized with short paragraphs or bullet points.
- **Closing**: Next steps, deadlines, or a clear statement that no action is needed.

## Restrictions

- Never include passwords, API keys, tokens, or other credentials in email bodies.
- Never include personal identification numbers (SSN, credit card numbers, etc.).
- If sensitive data must be shared, reference a secure channel or link instead.
